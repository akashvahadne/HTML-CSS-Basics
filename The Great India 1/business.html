Business Leaders


    Ratan Tata


Ratan Tata is one of the most respected and influential business leaders in India. He is the former chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group, a Mumbai-based conglomerate that operates in various sectors such as steel, automobiles, software, beverages, hotels, and more. He is also a renowned philanthropist who has donated billions of dollars to various causes, especially in the fields of education, health, and rural development. Here is some short information about his life and achievements: Ratan Tata was born on December 28, 1937 in Bombay (now Mumbai), during the British Raj. He is the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted by Ratanji Tata, the son of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. He has a younger brother Jimmy Tata and a half-brother Noel Tata. Ratan Tata studied at the Campion School and the Cathedral and John Connon School in Mumbai, the Bishop Cotton School in Shimla, and the Riverdale Country School in New York City. He graduated from Cornell University with a bachelor's degree in architecture in 1959. He later completed an advanced management program at Harvard Business School in 1975. Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1961 as a trainee on the shop floor of Tata Steel. He rose through the ranks and became the director in charge of National Radio and Electronics (NELCO) in 1971, where he turned around the struggling company. He became the chairman of Tata Industries in 1981 and succeeded his uncle J.R.D. Tata as the chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group in 1991. Under Ratan Tata's leadership, the Tata Group expanded its global presence and acquired several foreign companies such as Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, Corus Group, and Daewoo Motors. He also launched innovative products such as the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, and the Tata Swach, a low-cost water purifier. He also invested in emerging sectors such as e-commerce, biotechnology, and aerospace. Ratan Tata retired as the chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group in 2012 and was succeeded by Cyrus Mistry. However, he returned as the interim chairman in 2016 after Mistry was ousted by the board of directors. He finally stepped down in 2017 and handed over the reins to Natarajan Chandrasekaran. He continues to head the charitable trusts of the Tata Group, which own about 66% of Tata Sons. Ratan Tata is widely regarded as a visionary leader who transformed the Tata Group into a global powerhouse while maintaining its ethical values and social responsibility. He has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to business and society, such as the Padma Vibhushan (2008), Padma Bhushan (2000), Order of Australia (2023), Honorary Knight Grand Cross of the Order of the British Empire (2014), and Maharashtra Bhushan (2006). He is also an honorary fellow of several prestigious institutions such as the Royal Academy of Engineering, the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, and Cornell University.




    Adi Godrej

Here is some short information about him: Adi Godrej was born on April 3, 1942 in Mumbai, during the British Raj. He is the son of Naval Godrej, who was adopted by Burjorji Godrej, the son of Ardeshir Godrej, the founder of the Godrej Group. He has a brother Nadir Godrej and a cousin Jamshyd Godrej, who are also involved in the family business. Adi Godrej studied at St. Xavier's High School and St. Xavier's College in Mumbai, and then went to the United States to pursue higher education. He graduated from Cornell University with a bachelor's degree in architecture in 1959 and from MIT Sloan School of Management with an MBA in 1975. Adi Godrej joined the Godrej Group in 1961 as a trainee at Tata Steel, one of the group's companies. He rose through the ranks and became the director of National Radio and Electronics (NELCO) in 1971, where he revived the struggling company. He became the chairman of Tata Industries in 1981 and succeeded his uncle J.R.D. Tata as the chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group in 1991. He also launched innovative products such as the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, and the Tata Swach, a low-cost water purifier. He also invested in emerging sectors such as e-commerce, biotechnology, and aerospace. Adi Godrej retired as the chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group in 2012 and was succeeded by Cyrus Mistry. However, he returned as the interim chairman in 2016 after Mistry was ousted by the board of directors. He finally stepped down in 2017 and handed over the reins to Natarajan Chandrasekaran. He continues to head the charitable trusts of the Tata Group, which own about 66% of Tata Sons. Adi Godrej is widely regarded as a visionary leader who transformed the Tata Group into a global powerhouse while maintaining its ethical values and social responsibility. He is also an honorary fellow of several prestigious institutions such as the Royal Academy of Engineering, the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, and Cornell University.




   Azim Premji


Azim Hashim Premji is an Indian businessman and philanthropist who was the chairman of Wipro Limited. He is informally known as the Czar of the Indian IT Industry. He was responsible for guiding Wipro through four decades of diversification and growth, to finally emerge as one of the global leaders in the software industry. He has been regularly listed one among The 500 Most Influential Muslims. He was awarded Padma Vibhushan, India’s second-highest civilian award, by the Government of India. According to Forbes and Bloomberg Billionaires Index, Premji’s net worth is estimated at $9.3 billion and $25 billion, respectively, as of February 2023. In 2013, he agreed to give away at least half of his wealth by signing the Giving Pledge. Premji started with a $2.2 billion donation to the Azim Premji Foundation, focused on education in India. He topped the EdelGive Hurun India Philanthropy List for 2020.



   Mukesh Ambani


Mukesh Dhirubhai Ambani is an Indian billionaire businessman. He is currently the chairman and managing director of Reliance Industries, India’s most valuable company by market value. With an estimated net worth of $87.2 billion as of May 2023, he is the richest person in Asia and 13th richest in the world. He was born on 19 April 1957 in the British Crown colony of Aden (present-day Yemen) into a Gujarati Hindu family to Dhirubhai Ambani and Kokilaben Ambani.



  Lakshmi Mittal


Lakshmi Niwas Mittal is an Indian steel magnate based in the United Kingdom. He is the Executive Chairman of ArcelorMittal, the world’s second-largest steelmaking company, as well as chairman of stainless steel manufacturer Aperam. He was born on June 15, 1950 in Sadulpur, Rajasthan, India1. Mittal owns 38% of ArcelorMittal and holds a 3% stake in EFL Championship side Queens Park Rangers. In 2005, Forbes ranked Mittal as the third-richest person in the world, making him the first Indian citizen to be ranked in the top ten in the publication’s annual list of the world’s richest people. He was ranked the sixth-richest person in the world by Forbes in 2011 but dropped to 82nd place in March 2015. He is also the “57th-most powerful person” of the 72 individuals named in Forbes’ “Most Powerful People” list for 2015.
